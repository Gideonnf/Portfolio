## Technical Overview
**SIDM Sorting Pot** is an Augmented Reality (AR) application custom-built to streamline admissions engagement during the SIDM Open House 2019 event. Operating under a mythical sorting theme, prospective students select digital alchemy ingredients corresponding to distinct skill sectors (e.g., "Math," "Virtual Reality," "Drawing"). The AR application compiles these selections, dynamically mapping the user's explicit choices to recommended diploma pathways.

My role encompassed the total technical setup:
- **AR Component Tracking & Camera Projections**
- **Dynamic Skill-Filtering Algorithms**
- **UI Interaction Matrices in Screen-Space**

## About the Project
My club lecturers from my time in Nanyang Polytechnic's School of Interactive and Digital Media (SIDM) club approached us to help in digitalizing our Openhouse games. Both me and Sophie were assigned to a team and had came up with this Sorting Pot idea. We felt that the mythical theme behind the game suits SIDM and our lecturers were also on board with the idea so we went to implementing it. This project was challenging for a couple of reasons. I was still relatively new to Unity and AR Development and the game only had 3 days to be finished.

Fortunately, due to learning the basics in OpenGL, learning to use Unity was easy enough. AR Development in Unity had also been easier to pick up as they had built-in support for it. However, I still needed to crunch through three days to be able to finish the project in time for Openhouse. We managed to finish it in time and had received really good feedback on the Game. It had worked as intended and really helped visitors to find out more about our school and what courses they would be interested in.
