## Technical Overview
**SIDM Escape** is a Virtual Reality puzzle game engineered specifically for the SIDM Open House 2020 showcase. Built by a micro-team composed of two programmers and one artist, the layout relies entirely on an asymmetrical co-op loop heavily inspired by titles like *Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes*. The VR user solves physical micro-challenges within an immersive room by communicating with a real-world partner who interprets a physical instruction manual.

My layout contributions focused primarily on:
- **VR Spatial Interaction Pipelines** (Object grabbing, tracking, and physical triggers)
- **Asymmetric Logic State Puzzles**

## About the Project
My club lecturers from my time in Nanyang Polytechnic's School of Interactive and Digital Media (SIDM) club had approached my team and I to see if we were interested in doing one last project for SIDM Club before we graduated as we were all part of the Executive Committee. It helped that our course lecture allowed us to take up the project as our Final Year Project 2 as that meant that we could focus on it as part of our curriculum while helping our club. We were tasked to create a game that was simple enough to be played in a couple of minutes and was able to show case elements that could relate to SIDM's 6 courses. It was difficult, as we had to manage our other modules and our project had an earlier deadline than other Final Year Projects as we had to finish it by Openhouse.

We ultimately decided to work on a VR Puzzle game where we can incorpate the different courses in the puzzles itself. We took inspiration from Escape rooms and how they have to solve puzzles to be able to successfully escape the room. We had also incorpated similar Co-op elements from games such as "Keep talking and nobody explodes". Our puzzles had been designed for Co-op interaction between the person playing the game and someone outside of the game.
